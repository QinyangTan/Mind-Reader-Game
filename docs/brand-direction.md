# Mind Reader Brand Direction

## Shortlist

### 1. Moon Moth Parlor
- Midnight stage-show energy with a velvet curtain backdrop and gilded trim.
- Mascot is a moon moth oracle who feels original, memorable, and expressive.
- Gameplay surfaces mix warm parchment and dark theater framing for readable, nostalgic browser-game contrast.

### 2. Lantern Arcade
- Carnival-and-boardwalk palette with illuminated signwork and lucky-ticket details.
- Faster and more playful, but less mysterious.
- Strong ad placement fit, weaker sense of psychic ritual.

### 3. Star Cabinet
- Antique curiosity-cabinet styling with celestial drawers, charts, and brass instruments.
- Rich atmosphere, but it risks becoming more museum than game show.
- Great for archive screens, less strong for the moment-to-moment gameplay loop.

## Selected Direction

### The Moon Moth Parlor

Mind Reader becomes an original browser-game brand set inside a traveling moonlit parlor. The player enters a small stage world of velvet banners, parchment reading cards, brass trim, and pinned sponsor posters. The product keeps the guessing-game suspense, but the identity now comes from stagecraft instead of generic sci-fi panels.

## Brand Pillars

- Original, theatrical, playful, and slightly mysterious
- Readable and ad-aware without feeling like a cluttered portal
- Nostalgic browser-game energy with modern polish
- Recognizable mascot-driven identity rather than abstract chrome

## Mascot

### Mora

Mora is a velvet-winged moon moth oracle who hosts the game. She is not a genie and does not rely on borrowed folklore styling. She functions as the guide, emcee, and emotional anchor of the product.

Mood states used in the UI:
- inviting
- thinking
- confident
- celebrate
- surprised

## Visual System

- Backdrop: twilight plum, deep indigo, stage-light gold
- Primary surfaces: velvet panels with gilded trim
- Focus surfaces: parchment cards with dark ink
- Accent colors: brass gold, moonlight teal, rose-plum
- Decorative language: crescent-eye mark, moth wings, stars, playbill dividers, poster-like sponsor slots

## Ad Strategy

Ad placement is treated as part of the product shell rather than an afterthought.

Supported slots:
- top leaderboard banner
- right-side sponsor rail with 300x250-style modules
- bottom mobile playbill dock

Fallback behavior:
- branded house ads
- featured challenge cards
- sponsor placeholders that keep the layout balanced even without live ads
